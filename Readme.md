This repository contains a targeted virome capture panel designed to enrich viral nucleic acids from complex clinical and environmental samples prior to next-generation sequencing. The panel focuses on vertebrate-infecting DNA and RNA viruses and is optimized for broad coverage while maintaining high analytical sensitivity.

Built using a computationally condensed viral reference space, the panel targets dozens of viral families and hundreds of species with a non‑redundant probe set suitable for solution-based hybrid capture workflows. When coupled with standard metagenomic sequencing, this design enables several‑hundred‑fold increases in viral read fractions and markedly improved genome breadth and depth of coverage compared to unenriched libraries.

The panel is intended for:

comprehensive virome profiling in clinical, surveillance, and research settings,

sensitive detection of low‑abundance or mixed viral infections in high background host material,

characterization of both known viruses and divergent variants with substantial sequence mismatch to the original probe design.

You can adapt this to your exact implementation by adding:

the number of families/species targeted,

design pipeline details (reference databases, clustering thresholds),

and your recommended wet‑lab protocol (library prep, hybridization, and sequencing).
